Why this exists

Madeline's story

At 28, I had undergone 3 colonoscopies for symptoms I always assumed were related to Crohn's disease. Every biopsy came back normal. Meanwhile I was dealing with cysts on my ovaries at every ultrasound, painful and heavy periods, and bowel issues that had persisted for over 3 years.

"Manage your stress." That was the answer I kept getting. Until it wasn't good enough anymore.

After my colonoscopies, more than one doctor told me everything looked normal and I shouldn't come back until I was 40. So I was supposed to just live with this pain for another decade with no answers? That wasn't going to work.

It wasn't until I brought up the possibility of endometriosis to my OBGYN that someone finally validated the frustration and money I had poured into my health. I had already started pursuing pelvic floor therapy on my own — because I had to figure it out myself.

The lack of guidance during that process is what led me here. Nobody should have to navigate this alone. That's what The Informed Patient Co. is built to change.
